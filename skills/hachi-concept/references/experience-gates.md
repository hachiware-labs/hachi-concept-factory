# hachi-concept Experience Gates

Use this to test whether a concept is concrete enough to become a PRD.

## Scenario Gates

- First-use only product: make the first input, output, and activation moment extremely concrete; repeated use may be "none" only if the product is intentionally one-shot.
- Repeated-use product: explain what gets easier, richer, safer, or more valuable on the third and tenth use.
- Repository extraction: separate what the repo currently does from the product concept that could be built around it.
- Multi-SEED synthesis: choose one first adopter and keep rejected concepts as future SEEDs.
- Prompt-like concept: require durable artifact structure, local files, workflow state, evaluation, integration, or repeated review history.
- Dashboard-like concept: name the decision or action the user takes after seeing the output.

## 90-Point Output Gate

A strong hachi-concept output should satisfy all of these:

- Target user, target context, pain, product form, and wedge are narrow.
- Signature workflow includes first-use and repeated-use steps.
- Example input and example output are realistic enough for hachi-prd to derive use cases.
- Activation moment is observable, not just "the analysis is better".
- Sharpest non-goal blocks the most tempting scope creep.
- Differentiation is connected to the positioning brief and not only to style or model quality.
- Moat compounds through artifacts, workflow, context, integration, local-first behavior, Japanese context, process, or Agentable output.
- PRD readiness is blocked when any required experience field is vague.
